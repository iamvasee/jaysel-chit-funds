Common Questions about Chit Funds

Find answers to frequently asked questions about registered chit funds and Jaysel Chit Funds company in Tamil Nadu.

A chit fund is a savings and credit instrument where a group of people agree to contribute a fixed amount periodically. Members can bid for the collected amount, making it a flexible way to save and borrow.

Our chit funds are registered under the Chit Funds Act, 1982, and are regulated by the government. We maintain complete transparency and follow all legal requirements to ensure your investments are secure.

Monthly auctions are held where members can bid for the collected amount. The lowest bidder wins, and the difference between the total amount and the winning bid is distributed among all members as dividend.

Required documents include: ID proof (Aadhaar/PAN), address proof, income proof, and recent photographs. Additional documents may be required for higher-value chits.

Dividend earned from chit funds is taxable under 'Income from Other Sources'. However, businesses can claim subscription amounts as business expenses. Consult your tax advisor for detailed benefits.
